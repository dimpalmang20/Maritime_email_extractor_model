Speed / consumption claim are based on good weather conditions Beaufort
scale 4, Douglas scale 3 significant wave heights of 1.25m with no adverse
currents and the effect of the positive currents not to be debited against
the vessel. A good weather day is defined 24 hours consecutive good weather
only. "About" means having an allowance of -5% on speed and +5% on bunkers
consumption only. Extrapolation of good weather performance for bad weather
periods is not allowed.

 

Any evaluation of vessel's performance is to be done for the whole C/P
period including ballast and laden legs during periods of good weather
(periods of bad weather to be expressly excluded from calculations)
excluding maneuvers, periods before Start Of Sea Passage and periods after
End Of Sea Passage, steaming in rivers, confined waters and short sea
passages less than 24 hours from SOSP to EOSP.

                                                               

Any speed claim should give credit for bunkers saved (difference between
maximum quantities of bunkers the ship was allowed to consume during the
voyage and bunkers actually consumed) and vice versa any bunkers
overconsumption claim should give credit for time saved (difference between
maximum time the ship was allowed to complete the voyage and time actually
taken).
