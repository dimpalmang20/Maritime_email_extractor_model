WRITEUP
CAPESIZE
Activity remained subdued after market participants gradually returned from
the holiday season. This marked the second publishing day since the Capesize
benchmark vessel was changed to 182,000 dwt. However, the downward trend
persisted with the 5TC declining by $538 to settle at $27,652 today. The
North Atlantic remained quiet, while Brazil/West Africa stems with
end-January cancelling were circulated in the market, though no fixtures
were reported at the time of writing. Vessels with early February ETAs were
said to be attracting limited bidding interest. The C3 route of end
January/early February laycan was assessed at 0.064 below $22. In the
Pacific, fresh orders from both operators and miners entered the market, but
fixture levels fell further compared with last Friday. The C5 route settled
at $8.30, down 16 cents from last week. Rio Tinto was reported to have fixed
at $8.25 for 170,000/10 Dampier to Qingdao basis 19/21 January.
 
PANAMAX
Overall, the market had a muted start to the week, which was largely
expected on a Monday as most participants returned to their desks following
the festive break. Conditions on the fronthaul remain under some pressure,
however, the trans Atlantic market appears steadier, having found a more
stable trading range. In Asia, the owner sell-off seen toward the end of
last year has now largely thinned, with sentiment normalising and owners
showing firmer rate ideas, leading to improved numbers being discussed.
Cargo volumes remain relatively thin but with the larger and more modern
tonnage looking to ballast towards EC South America we may start to
experience some higher volatility across the board. The P5TC average posted
a $104 increase to publish at $11,640.

SUPRAMAX
A rather slow start to the week as players returned to their desks after the
recent holidays and took stock of the upcoming year. The Atlantic remained
lethargic with several European Countries looking forward to an additional
holiday tomorrow keeping fresh enquiry at a minimum. Elsewhere, both the
north and south Atlantic lacked much fresh impetus and rates remained around
last done. The Medi Atlantico (60,550 2016) open Abidjan 5/7 January was
heard to have recently been on subjects for a trans Atlantic run at $21,000
but no more details came to light. Also, the Xin Hai Tong 51 (56,607 2011)
open Cotonou 2/7 January was heard to have been fixed on a fronthaul to the
Far East at $17,000, again no more details surfaced. Asia remained negative,
brokers saying that there remained an abundance of prompt tonnage and the
cargo booked remained relatively restrained. The 11TC average fell $412 from
last Friday to start the first full week of the New Year at $13,189.
 
HANDYSIZE
The first week of the new year opened quietly, with rates in both basins
coming under pressure. The BHSI dropped a further 25 points to 660, while
the 7TC closed at $11,874. Across the Continent and the Mediterranean,
conditions remained weak, as owners either ballasting out of the region or
reassessing their expectations downward. Activity in the South Atlantic and
the US Gulf was also largely subdued, with freight levels declining across
most routes. Charterers continued to push bids lower, meeting resistance
from owners and resulting in a widening bid-offer gap. The Asian market
opened the week on a quiet note as well, with overall sentiment remaining
negative and charterers bidding below previously concluded levels. On the
period side, the Ocean Victory (28,386 2011) open Malaysia 13/16 January
reported fixed for one year with redelivery worldwide at $10,500, but
