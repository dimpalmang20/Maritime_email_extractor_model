TPM26 Cold Chain Insights

Container manufacturing in the refrigerated sector is soaring on the backs
of strong global cargo demand and continuing port congestion, especially in
Europe and parts of Asia. As JOC.com reported in late July, 93,000 TEU of
reefer equipment entered the global market in the first quarter of this
year, up almost 145% from a year earlier, according to Drewry. With port
congestion unlikely to subside anytime soon and with strong refrigerated
cargo demand in the US and globally, investment in and deployment of new
equipment is expected to continue through the end of the year. That's the
view of industry consultant Eskesen Advisory, the invaluable partner helping
to make TPM Cold Chain the premier global refrigerated conference.

Meanwhile, the administration of US President Donald Trump continues to be
aggressive on trade, creating uncertainty around tariffs while negotiating a
number of new trade agreements. All of this reflects a highly volatile
market that could go through multiple cycles by the time TPM26 rolls around.

Whatever uncertainty you may feel about the market today, you'll leave TPM26
Cold Chain better informed, better connected to a vast network of
like-minded professionals, and ready to tackle the challenges you confront.
