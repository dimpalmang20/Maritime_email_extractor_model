CAPESIZE
The Capesize market delivered a notably strong performance today, propelled
largely by pronounced tightness in the North Atlantic. The BCI 5TC surged by

upward pressure. The standout has undoubtedly been the trans-Atlantic
market, where exceptionally scarce tonnage and two very strong Seven Islands
fixtures set a bullish tone. This drove the C8 sharply higher by $16,625 to
$61,938, while the C9 climbed $7,111 to $70,611. In the Pacific, activity
was steady with two miners in the market, though both were resisting at the
time of writing, with offers reportedly in the high $12.00s. This
contributed to a firmer C5, which rose by 0.395 to $12.605. Momentum also
strengthened in the South Atlantic, where reports of $26 being paid on C3
and a firmer Brazil-Spain fixture supported sentiment. Bids for index dates

C3 index by 0.395 to $25.868.
