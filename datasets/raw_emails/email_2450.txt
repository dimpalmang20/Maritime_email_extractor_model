PANAMAX
The week opened quietly, with the seasonal wind-down toward Christmas
clearly underway and overall activity thinning across both the Atlantic and
Pacific basins. Market sentiment continued to drift lower amid a persistent
lack of fresh fixture activity. Trans-Atlantic and fronthaul rates remained
under pressure as charterers tested softer levels, with owner resistance
gradually easing. In Asia, conditions weakened further, with limited cargo
availability and little in the way of replenishment keeping activity
subdued. An oversupply of prompt tonnage continued to weigh on fundamentals,
enabling charterers to circulate increasingly aggressive rate ideas for
early cover. Period activity remained slim with only the Myrsini (82,117
