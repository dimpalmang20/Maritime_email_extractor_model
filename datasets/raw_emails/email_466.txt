CAPESIZE
The gains observed in the North Atlantic region since mid-last week have now
been fully reversed. With losses exceeding $4,000 on both trans Atlantic and
fronthaul routes, the Capesize 5TC has dropped back to $26,422, marking a
decline of $2,408 today. A fronthaul trip with two discharge ports in the
southern region was reported at a significantly lower level than the last
done. However, the vessel's redelivery location provided a positional
advantage, some commented. C9 subsequently returned to the $50,000 level,
undoing the surge seen last week. On the C3 route, the gap between offers
and bids appeared wide. However, following the Posco Newcastlemax fixture
with end-August cancelling, the C3 index showed a clearer downward trend,
approaching the $23 mark. In the Pacific, poor weather conditions persisted
in the CJK area with only one miner active in the market.

HANDYSIZE
It was a relatively more active day for the sector, though overall sentiment
remained flat and fundamentals were largely unchanged from yesterday. The
BHSI settled at 679, while the 7TC average rose by $31 to close at $12,223.
In the Continent and Mediterranean, brokers reported a continued flat trend
in rates due to a lack of fresh demand. The U.S. Gulf and South Atlantic
